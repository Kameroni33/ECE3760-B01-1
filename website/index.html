Vision Statement

Our aim is to facilitate non-auditory communication between skip and sweepers that is fast, reliable, and affordable helping to level the playing field for deaf curlers.

Design Criteria

  • Non-auditory signals
  • Fast communication
  • Intuitive use
  • Non-invasive design
  • Durable structure
  • Affordable product

Our Design Process

  1. Criteria & empathic design
  2. Brainstorming and problem solving
  3. Initial proof-of-concept prototype
  4. Physical prototype design
  5. Feedback and design iteration

Controller UI Design

  1. Joystick control of receiver devices
  2. LED indicators for visual feedback to the skip
  3. Buttons (to be removed)
  4. Mounting point for hand strap
  5. Removable battery cover for convenient and quick access

Receiver UI Design

  1. LED indicators for visual communication of commands
  2. Toggle switch to select LEFT/RIGHT side
  3. Power/pairing button
  4. Broom clap tightener to secure device in place
  5. Adjustable hinge for custom mounting angle
  6. Removable battery cover for convenient and quick access
